Waste of Time?

HTML Archive (2024)

Art 109 SJSU

Open project in new window

Project by Olivia Joy Cacdac and Alyssa Guardado

Time is tricky, and people ask the following questions: I wish I had more time to work on this. Or is the time going by so fast? In this archive, we take surveillance and reflect on the time we spent on two popular social media apps, Instagram and TikTok. We looked at the data from our phone's screen time and recorded it from August 27th through August 31st. This archive allows us to specifically look at how much time we spent on the social media app that day, and reflect on how else we could have spent that time. It brings attention to the problem of people feeling like they've wasted hours of their day scrolling through various short videos on social media. We can replace our time with a more productive way instead of using devices, such as grocery shopping, taking a power nap, watching a movie, cleaning your room, folding laundry, and many more ways. These comparisons bring the screen time into perspective. The database was collected by Alyssa Guardado and Olivia Joy Cacdac, who compared using both apps daily. It shows two people using the apps more than others or less than others. We included a date picker, radio buttons, and a text area in this archive to give viewers a space to record their own time spent on a social media app and encourage them to think about what else could be done in that amount of time. As a viewer, ask yourself this question. How much time are you giving away for free?
